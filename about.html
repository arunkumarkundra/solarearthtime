About the Saurapṛthvī Calendar & Jīvacakra Clock

Saurapṛthvī ("Solar Earth") is a reimagined calendar system based on Earth’s orbit and seasonal changes. It begins with a sacred Earth Day, Āditya, marking the March equinox and the start of the year. It honors Earth's natural rhythm rather than artificial divisions.

Month Names & Their Meanings

  • Avasra – Dawn (beginning of light and intention)
  • Bharna – Nourish (emergence and sustenance)
  • Chaitra – Awakening (activation of life)
  • Dharma – Order (balance and stability)
  • Elya – Growth (expansion and life force)
  • Falna – Blossom (fruition and beauty)
  • Gavya – Vitality (energy, power, and strength)
  • Harsha – Joy (peak expression, midsummer joy)
  • Ishra – Flow (inward turning and descent)
  • Janya – Renewal (restarting inner cycles)
  • Kshetra – Field (grounded productivity)
  • Loma – Shedding (release and letting go)

Earth Days – Their Names & Poetic Significance

  • Āditya – "Solar Origin" – the radiant gateway to a new year
  • Utsa – "Overflow" – the fullness of light at the summer solstice
  • Vimṛti – "Reflection" – a mirror in the middle of the year
  • Nidra – "Sacred Sleep" – the winter solstice, womb of renewal
  • Saṃbheda – "Union" – where endings fold into beginnings
  • Chāyopama – "The Shadow Twin" – a leap day that reminds us of the grace hidden in cosmic imperfection

Jīvacakra – The Living Clock

The Jīvacakra clock divides each day (Jīva) into 10 Prāṇas, representing vital phases like Awaken, Focus, Ease, and Dream. Each Prāṇa contains 100 Kālas (breath-like moments), and each Kāla contains 100 Tattvas (subtle pulses of awareness).
